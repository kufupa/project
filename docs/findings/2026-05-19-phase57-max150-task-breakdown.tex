\paragraph{Source.}
Results are from \texttt{MT50\_Phase57\_raw\_vs\_bounded\_decode\_25ep\_s1000\_max150\_nenv25\_true\_async\_3x1gpu}: three shards, 25 episodes per task, seed range 1000--1024, \texttt{n\_envs=25}, \texttt{max\_steps=150}, and \texttt{random\_seeded} MetaWorld resets.

\paragraph{Experiment setup.}
Phase57 audits whether SmolVLA actions look more realistic to a JEPA world model before or after action bounding. For each MetaWorld task, SmolVLA samples open-loop action chunks; the real environment is stepped with the raw postprocessed actions, while the world model is unrolled twice from the same real root state: once with the raw actions and once with the clipped/bounded actions.

Each episode uses \texttt{chunk\_len=50}: at every active observation, SmolVLA inference is batched across up to 25 parallel environments, samples a 50-step action chunk per environment, executes those actions until success, termination, or the 150-step cap, then samples the next chunk. Thus full-horizon failures usually have three chunks, while early successes have fewer.

The world model packs five environment actions per latent transition, so comparison columns align to real environment frames at steps 5, 10, \ldots, 50 within each chunk. Real frames and proprioception at those aligned indices are encoded into JEPA latents; raw and bounded world-model predicted latents are compared to the real latent with combined visual-plus-proprio L2. Lower L2 wins that column; ties are counted separately.
